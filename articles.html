Home Research Articles

On this page, we provide information about other professionals' research, development projects, etc. Here, professionals can link to their work if they want to inform a wider public about their work or about academic issues they are concerned about and wish to pass on.


  • Atypical sensory behavior in children with autism

    • Psychologist Lise Ankerstrøm, BUC Gentofte, as part of her specialist education in child psychology, has written an article on sensory processing in children with autism.

    In the article she makes a status of where one empirically is in relation to documenting the sensory specialties that children with autism experience. The sensory deviations occur - as something new - in proposals for the new diagnostic criteria for autism spectrum disorder in DSM-5. This makes it particularly interesting and relevant to get an overview of what we know about today and have empirical evidence regarding the particular sensory disorders in children with autism.

    You can read the finished article in:
    Educational Psychology Journal


  • Music therapy and autism

    • Ulla Holck, PhD, Associate Professor in Autism and Head of Center for Documentation and Research in Music Therapy, Aalborg University.

    Many children, adolescents and adults with autism, their relatives as well as professionals have learned that music therapy can make a difference to social skills, verbal and nonverbal communication and emotional interaction. Music therapy is thus an evidence-based treatment form, shown through Cochrane Reviews and RCT studies - also in relation to autism.

    Unfortunately, the knowledge of music therapy is not widespread, so the Center for Documentation & Research in Music Therapy, Aalborg University, has created the following website, www.cedomus.aau.dk , with updated knowledge about music therapy. In the section on autism, there is partly a Danish review article on research into music therapy with children with autism, and some mention and links to latest research and practice-related descriptions.

    Read more here:
    http://www.musikterapi.aau.dk/


  • Impairment of Social Function in Young Females With Recent-Onset Anorexia Nervosa and Recovered Individuals.

    • New Danish research project published in the Journal of Adolescent Health (2016):
    "Impairment of Social Function in Young Females With Recent-Onset Anorexia Nervosa and Recovered Individuals."

    Purpose:

    Anorexia nervosa (AN) displays social difficulties; HOWEVER, it is not clear if individuals with comorbid autism spectrum disorders account for these difficulties.

    By Mette Bentz, psychologist, PhD student, Child and Youth Psychiatric Center Department Bispebjerg

    Link: http://authors.elsevier.com/article/


  • Functioning and Disability in Autism Spectrum Disorder: A Worldwide Survey of Experts

    • In 2013, the Center for Autism participated in an expert panel at the request of the Center of Neurodevelopmental Disorders at Karolinska Institutet (KIND) in Sweden. It is a project where KIND aims to develop the WHO's International Classification of Function (ICF), so that it is useful for describing and assessing the characteristic functional problems of persons with autism.

    The result of this survey is now published in Autism Research, 2016 and can be downloaded here: http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/pdf

    Research article: Functioning and Disability in Autism Spectrum Disorder
